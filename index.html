Enterprise Mobility: Data-Driven Customer Retention Strategy

A Distinction-grade MSc Business Analytics capstone project at Swansea University, delivered for a real brief from Enterprise Mobility (Enterprise Rent-A-Car · National Car Rental). Mixed-methods analysis of 2,495 UK customers to diagnose churn drivers and produce a predictive retention roadmap.

Analytical Techniques Applied

📊
Multiple Regression Analysis
Identified loyalty drivers across brand KPIs — popularity and usage predict retention; short-term buzz has near-zero effect.
🔵
Cluster Segmentation (Power BI)
Segmented 2,495 respondents into behavioural groups — exposing the high-risk 18–25 cohort with 25% churn.
💬
NLP Sentiment Analysis
Natural language processing of open-text reviews. Negative sentiment = strongest single predictor of churn.
🗺️
Geospatial Heatmap Analysis
Customer satisfaction mapped by region — medium-density cities outperform megacities for retention and expansion.
📈
Tableau & Power BI Dashboards
Interactive KPI dashboards visualising churn drivers, retention patterns, media engagement, and demographic profiles.

Key Findings

  • ✦Repeated usage and brand popularity are the strongest loyalty predictors — buzz has limited or negative effect
  • ✦Negative online sentiment is the single most powerful predictor of churn — requires proactive reputation management
  • ✦18–25 age group: 25% churn vs 85%+ retention in 35–50 cohort — most acute strategic vulnerability
  • ✦AI-driven retention models can reduce churn by up to 15% (McKinsey, 2021)
  • ✦5% retention improvement raises profits by 25–95% — making targeted analytics intervention highly ROI-positive
Strategic Impact

Six-priority action plan with a 24-month phased roadmap — loyalty redesign, youth engagement, ML churn prediction, NLP sentiment monitoring, transparent pricing, and EV urban expansion.
